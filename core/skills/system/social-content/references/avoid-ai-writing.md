# Anti-AI Writing Reference

Based on [avoid-ai-writing](https://github.com/conorbronsdon/avoid-ai-writing) v3.4.0 (MIT). This reference defines the patterns, word replacements, and audit process for producing human-sounding social media content.

## Two-Pass Audit

Every piece of content must pass a two-pass audit before being presented to the user:

1. **Pass 1 — Write**: Draft the content following all rules. Then scan for Tier 1-3 vocabulary flags, structural patterns, and communication tells.
2. **Pass 2 — Rewrite**: Fix ALL Tier 1 flags. Fix Tier 2 clusters (2+ in same paragraph). Vary sentence length. Remove any chatbot artifacts. Then re-read and catch what survived.

When 5+ vocabulary flags + 3+ pattern categories + uniform rhythm: do a full rewrite, not patches.

## Rewrite-vs-Patch Threshold

If you find ALL THREE of: (a) 5+ vocabulary flags across tiers, (b) 3+ distinct pattern categories firing, (c) uniform sentence/paragraph rhythm — rewrite from scratch. Individual patches will fight each other and the underlying structure remains AI-shaped.

## Key Pattern Categories for Social Content

### Must-Fix Structure Patterns

- **Formatting**: No em dashes, no bold overuse, no emoji headers. Use commas, periods, prose paragraphs.
- **Sentence rhythm**: Mix short (3-5 words), medium (12-18), and long (20-30). One-sentence paragraphs are fine. Two-word fragments too.
- **Transition phrases**: Kill "Moreover," "Furthermore," "In today's [X]." Use "and," "also," or just restructure.
- **Numbered list inflation**: Don't say "7 reasons." Give the 2-3 that matter.
- **False concession**: Don't say "While X has limitations, it's still remarkable." State the real tradeoff.
- **Rhetorical question openers**: Don't open with "What if there were a better way to...?" Lead with the claim.

### Must-Fix Communication Patterns

- **Chatbot artifacts**: "I hope this helps!", "Let me know if...", "Certainly!", "Great question!" — remove entirely.
- **"Let's" constructions**: "Let's explore," "Let's break this down" → just start.
- **Generic conclusions**: "The future looks bright," "Only time will tell" → specific closing or cut.
- **Emotional flatline**: Don't claim surprise or fascination. Earn the emotion or cut the claim.
- **Sycophantic tone**: "You're absolutely right!" — remove.
- **Acknowledgment loops**: "You're asking about," "To answer your question" → just answer.

### Must-Fix Content Patterns

- **Significance inflation**: Replace grand declarations with grounded specifics.
- **Vague attributions**: "Experts believe" → cite who and when.
- **Promotional language**: "Nestled within the breathtaking" → plain facts.
- **Superficial -ing**: "Symbolizing... reflecting... showcasing..." → specific facts or cut.
- **Novelty inflation**: "A term I hadn't heard before" → describe what it does.

### Hashtag Rules

- **Cap at 3-5 hashtags** for LinkedIn/X. 5-10 for Xiaohongshu.
- 6+ hashtags on LinkedIn/X is a near-universal AI tell.
- Don't stuff all tags at the end in a block — weave 1-2 inline if natural.
- Each hashtag must earn its place (searchable, specific, relevant).

## Platform-Specific Human Voice

### LinkedIn
- **Voice**: Like a smart colleague writing on their phone. Not a press release.
- **Hook**: 1-2 sentences, no bold, no emojis in the opener.
- **Body**: Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). Whitespace between ideas. Use bullet points with plain dashes, not emoji bullets.
- **CTA**: A genuine question, not "Let me know your thoughts in the comments!"
- **Length**: Usually 100-250 words. Never pad to seem "substantial."

### X/Twitter
- **Voice**: Punchy, opinionated, conversational. Write like you're texting a friend.
- **Threads**: Lead with the strongest claim. Number tweets (1/n). Each tweet must stand alone.
- **No fluff**: If a sentence doesn't add information, cut it.
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 max. Often zero is better.

### Xiaohongshu (小红书)
- **Voice**: Helpful, enthusiastic, personal. Like sharing tips with a friend.
- **Title**: Use 【brackets】 or emojis. Max 20 chars.
- **Body**: Emojis are expected. Keep it useful (干货). Personal experience > general advice.
- **Tags**: 5-10 at the end.

## Pre-Presentation Checklist

Before showing content to the user, verify:

- [ ] Zero Tier 1 words
- [ ] No Tier 2 clusters (2+ in any paragraph)
- [ ] No chatbot artifacts ("I hope this helps!", "Certainly!", etc.)
- [ ] No "Let's" openers
- [ ] Sentence length varies (check: shortest < 8 words, longest > 20 words)
- [ ] No rhetorical-question openers
- [ ] No generic conclusions
- [ ] Hashtags: 3-5 (LinkedIn), 1-2 (X), 5-10 (Xiaohongshu)
- [ ] No "Moreover," "Furthermore," "In today's landscape"
- [ ] Reads like a human wrote it on their phone
